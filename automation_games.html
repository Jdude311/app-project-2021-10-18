Automation Games

Automation games are about automation and typically have multiple stages. You start off with very basic tools and resources and build your way up to unimaginably complex items. They focus more on replayability and the core gameplay compared to just grpahics or fast paced gameplay.

Factorio

Factorio is a highly polished game with streamlined elements and a great progression system. You start off by getting resources by hand, then you learn to mine resources autmatically, and refine those resources into more complex resources. If this sounds interesting to you go here on Steam.

Satisfactory

Satisfactory is an open world automation game. The game has no real goal except for automating everything and shipping all items to the space elevator. here on Steam.

Arma

If you're into milsim games--games that try to simulate real life as realistically as possible--then Arma is for you. The most current iteration, Arma III, has a massive sandbox and lots of physics. Buildings are destructible, weapons have attachments, and lots of vehicular combat: tanks, fighter jets, and APCs are all part of the game. Just beware: Arma has very heavy processor requirements.
